## Growth Strategy — Cross-Promotion (Phase 2+)

Once at scale (10+ channels live), accounts can be used for **legitimate cross-promotion**:

### ✅ Safe (within YouTube TOS)
- **Description links:** Each channel's description links to 1-2 related channels in the same niche
  - e.g., Finance channel → "Also watch: Crypto Confessions"
- **End screen promos:** Feature another owned channel on the last 5s of a video
- **Same-niche funneling:** Viewers of one channel are high-probability subs for a similar channel
- **Pinned comments from the channel itself:** "Check out our finance clips channel too 👇" — looks organic

### ❌ Risky — Do Not Do
**Fake engagement (likes/comments from owned accounts) is flaggable and not worth it:**
- All accounts originate from the same IP → instant cluster signal
- Even with proxies, hardware fingerprints + behavior timing expose coordination
- YouTube's ML looks for engagement not backed by watch time/retention
- Penalty hits the **target channel**, not just the fake accounts — suppressed reach or termination
- One terminated monetized channel = months of work wiped

### The math on why cross-promo beats fake engagement
- 50 fake likes → basically zero algo impact (YouTube ignores low-signal engagement)
- 50 real viewers funneled from a related channel → real retention → algo push
- A viewer who found you via description link is 10x more likely to subscribe than a passive Shorts scroller

### Channel Flip Strategy (exit before monetization)
- A gaming/finance Shorts channel with 1K+ subs + consistent views sells for $500-2K on Flippa / PlayerUp
- Faster cash than waiting for 10M Shorts views for YPP
- Cross-promotion accelerates sub growth → hits flip threshold faster
